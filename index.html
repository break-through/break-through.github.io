Background

An immigrant from Nepal, I came to the United States at a young age. Fascinated by Physics and Computers, I pursued my interests in these fields through high school at the 'School of Science and Engineering' in Dallas,TX. I graduated in 2017 to attend MIT, where I continued exploring those fields. From working with the International Students Association and re-founding the MIT Unmanned Aerial Vehicles with like-minded friends, to working on founding teams via Sandbox and DeltaV, I was able to explore my interests and gain new entrepeneurial insight. The classes I took there answered many questions I had - for example 6.824 (Distributed Systems) answered how the present global computer infrastructure was built, and 8.286 (Inflation Theory) answered deep questions on the Universe's formation. There were countless other amazing classes as well that taught me a lot about the modern world. I recently graduated in 2021 with a bachelors in both Computer Science and Physics. Other than that, I love to play Tennis, Soccer and Table Tennis, and work on Calisthenics. I am always interested in new technology, like Virtual or Augmented Reality. I enjoy astrophotography/space, making music, building drones, and learning about how to make machines 'think' on my free time. I also volunteer when I am able, and with coded projects if possible. At present, I'm doing my Master's thesis at MIT in the field of Reinforcement Learning. I've developed a deeper interest in statistics and am excited to keep on learning about all the awesome tools and technologies out there.

Follow me on ...

I really took 'social distancing' to heart starting with the Pandemic and reduced Social Media usage but I plan to start using more social media again in the distant-but-soon future, even though I personally prefer more personalized connections. Learning more about inflation theory has shown me how small the enormous echo chambers of social media really are, but studying computer science has shown me how technolgically amazing the tools can be. I'll find a nice balance in the years to come...
